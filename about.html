UNTETHERED MAGIC IS AN ORGANIC, SELF SUSTAINING RESIDENCY

About Untethered Magic

Untethered Magic is an organic, self-sustaining abode that provides an array of facilities. The space has numerous shared open-air spaces, which foster workshops, residencies, exhibitions, documentation, and other collaborative projects.

The space can cater to up to 40 people, as it’s fully equipped with catering facilities and a contemporary kitchen. Among other facilities include digital amenities, where the residents and visitors can broadcast, take photos, record music, shoot vlogs & cinematic videos that can be edited and processed on Untethered Magic’s workstation. We also provide an art studio and library that make it possible to research, conceptualize and produce contemporary or personal work.

Untethered Magic Residencies

Untethered Magic, formed in August 2019 by Syowia Kyambi, Kibe Wangunyu and Kiberu Dennis, is a home as well as a sanctuary; a safe space for creatives focusing on supporting the process for research based conceptual contemporary arts. Our oasis is a self-made space which hosts and grows residencies and individual practices for occupants and visitors. It’s a space that encourages growth and independence, learning, unlearning and relearning space, process, life and art. Untethered Magic is a re-imagination of living human relationships, a supportive communal space while also being defiant to the normal societal structure specific to the context of Kenya.

We hosted our first large scale residency accommodating thirty four people in January 2020, in collaboration with the Centre for Art, Design and Social Research and their 2019-2020 international fellows facilitating an open call for the East African community titled De/Archive. Previous to this we hosted two young artists Nikomambo Odihambo and Virginia Wakianda. These residencies were created out of the artists need to temporarily escape their daily lives and have time and space to immerse themselves into their process taking a break from their daily lives.
